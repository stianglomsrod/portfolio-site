# DNB Positioning

## Target role
Software Engineer, AI-First Engineering — DNB AI Tech.

## Candidate thesis
Stian is not applying as a conventional senior platform engineer. He is applying as an AI-first builder / software engineering candidate with unusually strong cross-domain system understanding and documented learning velocity.

He combines:
- programming curiosity
- practical fullstack framework awareness
- AI-assisted prototyping and development
- UX/product/user-centered design competence
- participatory design and digital learning design
- project leadership and documentation discipline
- ability to help others understand and use new tools

## What to emphasize
### AI-first builder, not AI magician
Stian uses AI agents as development partners, but does not outsource judgment. He provides context, evaluates output, catches wrong assumptions, asks for tests/QA, documents decisions, and connects technical choices to user needs.

### System understanding
Emphasize practical understanding of:
- frontend
- backend
- APIs
- databases
- auth
- frameworks
- CSS libraries
- deployment-oriented product structure
- how these layers fit together in real products

### High sustained learning/work capacity
Frame this professionally as:
- high learning velocity
- sustained output over time
- ability to integrate UX, development, research, and AI-assisted workflows
- ability to move from unfamiliar concepts to working prototypes
- persistence in debugging, documenting, testing, and iterating

Avoid private framing, health references, or claims like genius/superhuman/world-class.

### Research-based product/design strength
Stian has research-based competence in:
- participatory design
- user-centered design
- digital learning design
- innovation
- prototyping
- project management

This matters for AI-first engineering because AI agents need clear problem framing, constraints, context, and evaluation criteria.

### Enablement
DNB emphasizes helping other engineers become more productive. Stian's teaching background and documentation habits are relevant because he can turn complex workflows into understandable guides, runbooks, demos, and learning material.

## Evidence to use
- Klar platform/prototype.
- VG X portfolio site.
- AI-assisted prototyping and development workflow.
- Markdown-based agent onboarding and handover docs.
- QA docs, code audits, tech debt logs, verification reports.
- CS50x and programming learning trajectory.
- Fullstack/web prototype experience with frameworks and APIs.
- Interest ranging from web/fullstack to low-level C and sensor/real-world-interaction projects.

## What not to say
- Do not claim senior distributed systems/platform engineering expertise.
- Do not present Stian as only UX.
- Do not present Stian as only a prompt engineer.
- Do not use vague AI hype.
- Do not say or imply the DNB page is official DNB material.
- Do not mention private health or psychological context.

## Suggested tone
Concrete, curious, technically honest, warm, and slightly bold. The page/application should feel like a human builder with unusual learning drive, not a corporate AI brochure.
